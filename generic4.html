Trichoderma harzianum

The Genus Trichoderma is one of the most abundant fungi that have been shown to be present in all climatic zones. As such, it can be found in virtually all soils or rotting wood given that they are found in the roots of plants. However, this fungi can also be found on various parts of plants including the leaves, seeds and grains.

One of the most unique traits of this genus, compared to other fungi, is that they tend to attack other types of fungi. This has resulted in Trichoderma fungi being labeled as antagonists to other types of fungi. While it can be found living in symbiosis with plants (in the roots of plants) they have also been shown to exist as parasites in plants and a variety of other organisms.
